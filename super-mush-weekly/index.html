Weekly Insights & Actions
►
Weekly Highlights — Super Mush generated $7,963 in total sales across 273 units sold for the week of July 6–12, with Wednesday July 8 emerging as the peak sales day. PPC contributed $2,553 of total revenue, representing roughly 32% of sales driven through paid channels. Overall performance reflects a normalization period following Amazon Prime Day (July 8–9, 2026), which anchored mid-week volume around the peak day.
►
Metrics to Watch — The prior week included Amazon Prime Day (July 8–9, 2026), and the -57.7% WoW decline is expected post-event normalization, not a cause for alarm. TACoS at 46.1% and ACOS at 140.0% indicate that ad spend is significantly outpacing the revenue it generates, which warrants close monitoring as organic velocity rebalances post-Prime Day. ROAS of 0.71x confirms that for every dollar spent on ads, only $0.71 was returned, making efficiency optimization the priority focus heading into the following week.
►
Ad Spend Efficiency — Ad spend of $3,569 against PPC revenue of $2,553 resulted in a net ad loss of $1,016, signaling that current bids and targeting are not converting efficiently enough to justify the spend level. CTR of 0.68% is relatively low, suggesting ad creatives or placements may not be compelling enough to drive clicks at scale. CVR of 5.45% is a relative bright spot, meaning traffic that does land on the listing is converting at a reasonable rate, so the focus should shift toward improving click quality over raw volume.
►
Actions This Week — Audit and reduce bids on high-spend, low-converting keywords to bring ACOS down from 140.0% toward a target closer to 60–70% over the next two weeks. Run a search term report to identify wasted spend and shift budget toward the top-performing terms that contributed to Wednesday's peak day performance. Test updated ad creative or main image variations to improve CTR beyond the current 0.68% baseline and attract higher-quality traffic to the listing.
