Weekly Insights & Actions
►
Weekly Highlights — Lewie posted $290 in total sales across 11 units sold during Week 28 (July 5–11), marking a strong +176.2% week-over-week increase. Monday July 6 was the peak sales day, suggesting early-week momentum that could be leveraged going forward. PPC contributed $110 of the $290 in total revenue, meaning organic and other channels accounted for the remaining $180.
►
Metrics to Watch — ROAS came in at 0.49x, meaning the brand is generating less than $0.50 for every $1.00 spent on ads, which is well below a sustainable threshold. TACoS at 77.7% indicates that ad spend is consuming a disproportionate share of total revenue and warrants immediate attention. These two metrics together signal that the current ad investment is not yet paying back efficiently relative to overall sales volume.
►
Ad Spend Efficiency — $225 was spent on ads to drive $110 in PPC revenue, resulting in an ACOS of 2.0% — however, the ROAS of 0.49x reveals a disconnect worth investigating in how total attributed revenue is being calculated or segmented. CTR of 0.89% and CVR of 4.45% are reasonable conversion signals, suggesting the product page and targeting are functional but that bid levels or campaign structure may be inflating spend without proportional return. Reducing wasted spend on low-performing keywords or placements could meaningfully improve ROAS this week.
►
Actions This Week — Audit active campaigns to identify keywords with high spend and low or zero attributed sales, and pause or reduce bids on those terms immediately. With a CVR of 4.45% showing solid conversion, shifting budget toward top-converting search terms could help improve ROAS from 0.49x toward a breakeven target of 1.0x or higher. Consider setting a daily ad budget cap to prevent spend from outpacing revenue while optimization work is underway.
