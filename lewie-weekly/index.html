Weekly Insights & Actions
►
Weekly Highlights — Lewie posted $330 in total sales across 14 units for Week 29, reflecting a solid 13.8% week-over-week gain with Friday July 17 standing out as the peak sales day. PPC drove $190 of that revenue, meaning a meaningful portion of sales came through paid traffic, though organic contribution remains an area to develop further.
►
Metrics to Watch — ACOS at 144.8% and ROAS at 0.69x are the most pressing concerns this week, signaling that ad spend is significantly outpacing the revenue it generates. TACoS of 83.4% reinforces that paid advertising is carrying a heavy load relative to total sales, and these figures will need to trend downward to move toward profitability.
►
Ad Spend Efficiency — $275 in ad spend against $190 in PPC revenue represents a net ad loss of $85, making efficiency improvements the highest priority right now. CTR of 0.67% suggests the listings or ad creatives may not be compelling enough at the impression level, while CVR at 3.94% is a relative bright spot indicating that shoppers who do click are converting at a reasonable rate.
►
Actions This Week — Audit the highest-spend, lowest-converting keywords and pause or reduce bids on any terms burning budget without producing sales. Test updated ad creative or main images to improve CTR above 1%, which would stretch the existing spend further without increasing the budget. Set a weekly ad spend cap closer to $200 to protect margin while conversion and targeting optimizations are being implemented.
