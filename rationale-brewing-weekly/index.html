Weekly Insights & Actions
►
Weekly Highlights — RationAle Brewing posted $4,918 in total sales across 80 units sold during Week 29, representing a solid 10.5% week-over-week increase. Friday, July 17 was the peak sales day, suggesting strong end-of-week momentum that can be leveraged in future scheduling. The CVR of 16.23% is a genuine bright spot, indicating that shoppers who reach the listing are converting at a healthy rate.
►
Metrics to Watch — ACOS of 95.7% and a ROAS of just 1.05x signal that paid advertising is barely breaking even on revenue generated, with $1,582 in ad spend returning only $1,654 in PPC revenue. TACoS at 32.2% further confirms that ad costs are consuming a disproportionate share of total revenue. These figures warrant close attention to ensure the business is building toward profitable scaling rather than subsidizing growth at a loss.
►
Ad Spend Efficiency — With $1,582 in ad spend driving only $1,654 in PPC revenue, there is minimal margin buffer in the current campaign structure. A CTR of 0.53% suggests that ads are not compelling enough clicks at the impression level, pointing to potential issues with creative, keyword targeting, or bid positioning. Reallocating budget toward the highest-converting keywords and pausing underperforming targets could meaningfully improve ROAS from its current 1.05x baseline.
►
Actions This Week — Audit all active PPC campaigns to identify keywords with high spend and low conversion, pausing or reducing bids on any term exceeding a 70% ACOS threshold. Test updated ad creative or main image variations to address the low 0.53% CTR and improve top-of-funnel efficiency. Capitalize on Friday's proven peak-day performance by scheduling any promotions, coupons, or deals to activate Thursday evening through Friday to maximize conversion volume.
