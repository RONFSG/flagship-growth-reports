Weekly Insights & Actions
►
Weekly Highlights — Opopop closed Week 28 with $47,149 in total sales across 774 units, anchored by a strong peak on Saturday, July 11. PPC contributed $18,085 in revenue at an impressive 5.18x ROAS, signaling that paid traffic is converting efficiently. CVR of 20.02% reflects strong product-page performance and consumer intent among shoppers reached by ads.
►
Metrics to Watch — Total sales declined 3.6% week-over-week, a modest dip that warrants monitoring but does not indicate a structural issue at this stage. CTR of 0.79% is the most actionable concern, as it suggests ad creative or keyword targeting may not be compelling enough to consistently drive clicks at scale. Improving click volume while maintaining the current 20.02% CVR would be the most direct lever to recover and grow top-line revenue.
►
Ad Spend Efficiency — At $3,489 in ad spend, TACoS of 7.4% and ACOS of 19.3% reflect a healthy and well-managed paid media structure relative to total revenue. The gap between TACoS and ACOS confirms that organic sales are contributing meaningfully alongside PPC, reducing overall dependence on paid channels. There is room to strategically increase spend on high-converting placements without materially disrupting efficiency ratios.
►
Actions This Week — Audit and refresh ad creative and headline copy to address the 0.79% CTR, prioritizing the top-performing ad groups that drove Saturday's peak sales. Test bid increases on converting keywords to capitalize on the strong 20.02% CVR and push for higher impression share where ROAS justifies additional investment. Monitor daily sales pacing against last week's Saturday peak to identify which day-of-week patterns can be reinforced through dayparting adjustments.
