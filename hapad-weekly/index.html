Weekly Insights & Actions
►
Weekly Highlights — Hapad closed Week 29 with $16,434 in total sales across 573 units, with Friday July 17 serving as the strongest day of the week. PPC drove $9,551 of that revenue, reflecting heavy reliance on paid traffic to sustain volume. CVR held strong at 16.07%, signaling that shoppers who reached the listing were converting at a healthy rate.
►
Metrics to Watch — Total sales declined 7.3% week-over-week, a movement worth monitoring as no confirmed tentpole event explains the pullback. ACOS at 46.4% is running well above a sustainable efficiency threshold, meaning paid sales are coming at a high cost relative to revenue generated. TACoS at 27.0% further reinforces that ad spend is consuming a significant share of total topline, warranting closer review.
►
Ad Spend Efficiency — With $4,434 in ad spend producing a 2.15x ROAS, there is meaningful room to improve return before scaling further. The gap between ACOS at 46.4% and a typical target range of 25–30% suggests bids on lower-performing keywords may be inflating costs without proportional revenue contribution. Auditing search term reports to identify and reduce spend on high-spend, low-conversion terms should be a priority this week.
►
Actions This Week — Pull the search term report and pause or reduce bids on any keyword with ACOS above 60% and fewer than three orders over the past 14 days. With CTR sitting at 0.56%, test a main image variation or headline update to improve click-through and reduce the cost-per-click burden. Set a daily budget cap review to ensure ad spend does not outpace revenue gains as Week 30 progresses.
