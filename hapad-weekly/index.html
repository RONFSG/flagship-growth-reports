Weekly Insights & Actions
►
Weekly Highlights — Hapad posted a strong Week 28 with $17,732 in total sales across 536 units sold, marking a +7.5% week-over-week gain. Thursday, July 9 was the peak sales day, suggesting mid-week demand concentration. PPC drove $9,551 of total revenue, confirming paid traffic as a primary growth lever this period.
►
Metrics to Watch — ACOS at 48.7% and TACoS at 26.2% both sit at elevated levels that warrant closer review, as nearly one in four total revenue dollars is being consumed by ad spend. CTR of 0.60% indicates that a significant portion of impressions are not converting to clicks, pointing to potential gaps in creative or keyword targeting relevance. These two metrics together suggest the funnel has room for improvement before the top-line growth becomes fully profitable.
►
Ad Spend Efficiency — With $4,652 in ad spend generating a ROAS of 2.05x, the return is positive but leaves meaningful room for optimization given the high ACOS of 48.7%. CVR of 16.29% is a strong signal that shoppers who do click are converting well, meaning the inefficiency lies more in click acquisition cost than in listing performance. Tightening bids on underperforming keywords and reallocating toward high-CVR ASINs or placements could improve ROAS without sacrificing volume.
►
Actions This Week — Audit campaigns with ACOS above 55% and reduce bids or pause low-performing keywords to bring overall ACOS closer to the 35–40% target range. Test updated ad creative or main images to address the 0.60% CTR, as even a modest improvement to 0.75–0.80% could meaningfully reduce cost-per-click at current spend levels. Leverage the strong Thursday performance data to increase intraday bid adjustments mid-week and capture demand during peak conversion windows.
