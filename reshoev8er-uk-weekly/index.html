Weekly Insights & Actions
►
Weekly Highlights — Reshoev8er UK closed Week 28 with £3,477 in total sales across 71 units, driven by a strong Wednesday July 8 peak day. PPC contributed £1,448 of that revenue, showing paid traffic remains a meaningful share of the overall sales mix. The -8.4% WoW decline warrants monitoring but does not indicate a structural issue at this stage.
►
Metrics to Watch — ACOS at 63.4% is significantly above a healthy threshold, meaning paid campaigns are consuming a disproportionate share of revenue relative to returns. TACoS at 26.4% confirms that ad spend is heavily influencing total sales, leaving limited organic contribution to cushion costs. CTR at 0.10% is also critically low and suggests ad creatives or targeting may not be resonating with the intended audience.
►
Ad Spend Efficiency — With £917 in ad spend generating a ROAS of only 1.58x, the current return on paid investment leaves little margin for profitability after product and operational costs. The gap between ACOS (63.4%) and a target range of 20-35% indicates campaigns may be bidding too aggressively on low-converting keywords or broad match terms. Consolidating spend toward the highest-converting ASINs and search terms would help bring ACOS into a more sustainable range.
►
Actions This Week — Audit all active PPC campaigns and pause any keywords with ACOS above 80% while reallocating that budget toward proven converters. Work to improve CTR from 0.10% by testing new ad creative, updated main images, or revised ad copy that better communicates product value. Set a weekly ad spend cap aligned to a target ROAS of 2.5x or higher to enforce discipline and protect margin through the remainder of July.
