Weekly Insights & Actions
►
Weekly Highlights — Tubr Storage closed Week 29 with $3,336 in total sales across 74 units, with Monday July 13 standing out as the peak sales day. PPC drove $1,819 of that revenue, meaning organic and other channels contributed roughly $1,517, showing a reasonable split between paid and non-paid demand. The -13.5% WoW decline is worth monitoring but does not indicate a structural breakdown in the business.
►
Metrics to Watch — ACOS at 52.5% is the most pressing concern this week, sitting well above a healthy target range and indicating that ad spend is not converting efficiently enough relative to revenue generated. TACoS at 28.6% reinforces this, as it reflects a high ad cost burden across total sales, not just PPC revenue. These two figures together suggest the current bidding or targeting strategy needs review before spend scales further.
►
Ad Spend Efficiency — With $955 in ad spend generating $1,819 in PPC revenue at a 1.90x ROAS, there is room to improve returns before increasing budget. A CVR of 3.17% is functional but not strong, and paired with a 1.32% CTR, it points to either weak listing conversion elements or traffic being driven from loosely matched keywords. Tightening keyword targeting and improving listing content could lift both CVR and ROAS without requiring additional spend.
►
Actions This Week — Audit the top spending keywords and pause or reduce bids on any term with ACOS above 70% to bring the overall ACOS closer to the 35-40% range. Review the product listing for Tubr Storage — specifically the hero image, bullet points, and price positioning — to identify friction points suppressing the 3.17% CVR. Set a weekly ad spend cap slightly below $955 until ACOS improves, protecting margin while optimization work is underway.
