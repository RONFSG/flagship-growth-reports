Weekly Insights & Actions
►
Weekly Highlights — Dormeo Canada closed Week 27 with $68,753 in total sales across 135 units, driven by a strong Sunday July 5 peak day. ROAS came in at an impressive 9.72x against $4,673 in ad spend, with PPC contributing $44,783 of total revenue. Despite the week-over-week decline, the underlying efficiency metrics remain healthy and reflect a stable advertising foundation.
►
Metrics to Watch — The -11.2% WoW decline is not a cause for concern — the prior week included Amazon Prime Day (June 23–26, 2026), and this pullback is expected post-event normalization as elevated demand returns to baseline. CTR at 0.33% and CVR at 2.39% are the metrics that warrant closer attention, as improving click-through rates could unlock additional volume without requiring heavier spend. Monitoring these conversion funnel metrics over the next two weeks will help confirm whether Week 27 represents a stable new baseline or a temporary dip.
►
Ad Spend Efficiency — TACoS at 1.0% and ACOS at 10.6% indicate that ad spend is exceptionally well-controlled relative to total revenue, leaving meaningful room to scale without sacrificing margin. The gap between ACOS and TACoS suggests strong organic sales contribution alongside PPC, which is a healthy signal. There is an opportunity to incrementally increase bids on top-converting keywords to capture more volume while staying well within efficiency thresholds.
►
Actions This Week — Run a search term audit on PPC campaigns to identify high-impression, low-CTR keywords and test updated ad copy or imagery to push CTR above 0.40%. Given the post-Prime Day normalization, consider launching a targeted retargeting campaign or a modest deal to re-engage shoppers who browsed during Prime Day but did not convert. Set a weekly spend ceiling of $5,200 to test modest budget expansion while protecting the strong TACoS of 1.0%.
