Weekly Insights & Actions
►
Weekly Highlights — Dormeo Canada closed Week 28 with $55,197 in total sales across 108 units, supported by $42,740 in PPC revenue and an exceptional ROAS of 10.37x. Wednesday July 8 was the peak sales day, indicating mid-week demand concentration. Overall performance remains strong on an efficiency basis despite the week-over-week shift.
►
Metrics to Watch — The -33.2% WoW decline is not a cause for concern — the prior week included Amazon Prime Day (July 8–9, 2025 equivalent cycle), and this pullback reflects expected post-event normalization in consumer purchasing. CTR at 0.35% and CVR at 2.44% are the metrics worth monitoring more closely, as improving click-through rate in particular would compound the already healthy conversion rate. These should be the focus of ongoing creative and targeting reviews heading into the back half of July.
►
Ad Spend Efficiency — With TACoS at 1.1% and ACoS at 10.2% against $4,206 in ad spend, Dormeo Canada is operating with strong cost discipline relative to total revenue generated. The gap between PPC revenue ($42,740) and total sales ($55,197) also suggests meaningful organic contribution, which is a healthy signal worth protecting. There is room to modestly scale spend if bids and budgets are increased on top-converting ASINs without materially disrupting ACoS.
►
Actions This Week — Review Wednesday July 8 performance by ASIN and campaign to identify which products and ad types drove the peak, then apply those learnings to boost Tuesday–Thursday targeting. Test updated ad creatives or headline copy aimed at lifting CTR above 0.40%, as even a modest improvement would meaningfully increase impression-to-sale efficiency at current CVR levels. Monitor daily pacing closely to ensure ad budgets are not exhausting before peak shopping hours mid-week.
