Weekly Insights & Actions
►
Weekly Highlights — Opopop closed Week 29 with $45,799 in total sales across 428 units, reflecting solid baseline performance for the period of July 12–18. Saturday July 18 stood out as the peak sales day, suggesting strong weekend demand worth monitoring for future promotional timing. Conversion rate held at an impressive 18.86%, indicating that shoppers who found the listings were highly motivated to purchase.
►
Metrics to Watch — Total sales dipped 2.9% week over week, a modest decline that warrants monitoring but does not signal an alarming trend at this stage. CTR at 0.76% remains the clearest area of opportunity, as low click-through rates suggest ad creatives or listing imagery may not be capturing enough attention in search results to drive incremental traffic volume.
►
Ad Spend Efficiency — With a ROAS of 5.16x and a TACoS of 4.1%, ad spend is performing efficiently relative to total revenue, meaning paid traffic is well-integrated with organic sales. ACOS at 19.4% is within a healthy range for most consumer brands, though there may be room to tighten bids on underperforming keywords to bring it closer to the 15–17% range without sacrificing volume. The $1,891 in ad spend generating $9,756 in PPC revenue confirms strong return on paid investment this week.
►
Actions This Week — Audit ad creatives and main listing images to address the 0.76% CTR, testing at least one new hero image or headline variation to improve click volume from search. Identify the top-converting keywords driving the 18.86% CVR and increase bids on those terms to capture additional share before the weekend peak. Consider scheduling a targeted promotion or deal leading into Saturday to capitalize on the observed weekend demand pattern established in Week 29.
