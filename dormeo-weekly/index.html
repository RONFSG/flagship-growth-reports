Weekly Insights & Actions
►
Weekly Highlights — Dormeo closed the July 6–12 week with $49,042 in total sales across 121 units sold, driven by a strong mid-week peak on Wednesday, July 8. PPC contributed $19,842 of total revenue, reflecting healthy paid channel engagement. ROAS of 5.96x and TACoS of 8.2% indicate solid overall advertising efficiency relative to total business volume.
►
Metrics to Watch — The prior week included Amazon Prime Day (July 2026), and the -59.8% WoW decline is expected post-event normalization, not a cause for concern. CTR of 0.43% and CVR of 2.01% are areas worth monitoring, as improvements to either could meaningfully increase PPC revenue without requiring additional spend. Tracking these conversion metrics closely over the next two weeks will establish a reliable post-Prime Day baseline for Dormeo.
►
Ad Spend Efficiency — At $3,986 in ad spend, ACOS sits at 20.5%, which is acceptable but leaves room for optimization to push closer to a 15–18% target range. The gap between TACoS at 8.2% and ACOS at 20.5% confirms that organic sales are carrying meaningful weight alongside paid, which is a strong structural signal. Focusing budget toward the highest-converting placements, particularly those that drove Wednesday's peak, could improve ACOS without sacrificing volume.
►
Actions This Week — Audit the top PPC campaigns that contributed to the Wednesday, July 8 peak and identify which keywords or placements drove the highest CVR to prioritize budget allocation. With post-Prime Day traffic normalizing, reduce bids on broad match terms where ACOS exceeded 25% during the July 6–12 period to protect margin. Run a CTR improvement test on the top three ASINs by impression share, focusing on main image or title refinements to push CTR above the 0.50% threshold.
