Weekly Insights & Actions
►
Weekly Highlights — Dormeo generated $122,099 in total sales with 321 units sold during the June 29–July 5 week, with Sunday July 5 emerging as the strongest single day. PPC contributed $58,548 in revenue at a strong 4.65x ROAS, signaling that paid traffic remained highly productive despite overall volume softening. TACoS of 10.9% reflects a healthy ad-to-total-sales ratio and confirms efficient spend relative to the full business.
►
Metrics to Watch — The prior week included Amazon Prime Day (June 23–26, 2026), and the -53.1% WoW decline is expected post-event normalization, not a signal of underlying business deterioration. CVR of 2.17% and CTR of 0.44% are the metrics most worth monitoring this week, as both sit below typical top-performer benchmarks and represent real conversion efficiency opportunities. Tracking these over the next 7–14 days will establish a clean post-Prime Day baseline.
►
Ad Spend Efficiency — The $13,192 in ad spend produced a 23.9% ACOS, which is acceptable but leaves room for tightening as the business returns to organic velocity post-Prime Day. With ROAS at 4.65x, top-performing campaigns are clearly working — the priority should be identifying lower-ROAS placements dragging the blended average and reallocating that budget. A spend audit focused on exact-match vs. broad-match efficiency could yield measurable ACOS improvement within two weeks.
►
Actions This Week — Audit campaigns by ACOS at the keyword level and pause or reduce bids on any term exceeding 35% ACOS to bring the blended rate closer to 18–20%. Run a CVR diagnostic on the top 5 ASINs to assess whether listing content, pricing, or review count is suppressing conversion rate below its potential. Use Sunday July 5 performance data to inform dayparting decisions and ensure budgets are not exhausted before peak daily traffic windows.
