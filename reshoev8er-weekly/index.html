Weekly Insights & Actions
►
Weekly Highlights — Reshoev8er closed Week 28 with $16,658 in total sales across 531 units, supported by $8,801 in PPC revenue and a strong ROAS of 4.40x. Wednesday July 8 stood out as the peak sales day, suggesting mid-week demand momentum worth tracking into Week 29. Overall performance remains structurally sound given the post-event context driving the volume pullback.
►
Metrics to Watch — The prior week included Amazon Prime Day (June 23–26, 2026), and the -49.2% WoW decline is expected post-event normalization, not a cause for concern. CTR of 0.50% is the metric most worth monitoring, as it indicates ads are receiving impressions but generating limited click engagement relative to potential. CVR at 17.77% is a genuine bright spot and confirms that shoppers who do click are converting at a healthy rate.
►
Ad Spend Efficiency — With $2,010 in ad spend delivering a 4.40x ROAS and a TACoS of 12.2%, the account is operating efficiently in a lower-volume week. ACOS at 23.2% sits at an acceptable level but leaves room for optimization, particularly if CTR can be improved to bring down cost-per-click contribution. Maintaining disciplined spend this week while demand normalizes will protect margin heading into the back half of Q3.
►
Actions This Week — Audit low-CTR campaigns and refresh ad creative or main images to improve click engagement and push CTR above 0.60%. Review Wednesday July 8 performance data to identify which ASINs or ad types drove the peak and replicate that structure across the remainder of the week. Hold ad spend steady around the $2,000–$2,100 range while monitoring ACOS daily to ensure it does not creep above 25% during the normalization period.
