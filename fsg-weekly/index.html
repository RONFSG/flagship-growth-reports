Weekly Insights & Actions
►
Weekly Highlights — FSG generated $146,110 in total sales across 4,061 units in Week 28, with a blended ROAS of 5.83x and $67,196 in PPC-driven revenue. The -57.0% WoW decline is expected post-event normalization following Amazon Prime Day (June 23–26, 2026), which inflated the prior week's baseline. Dormeo led all brands at $49,042, followed by Sherpa Brands at $33,108 and Opopop at $20,463.
►
Metrics to Watch — The prior week included Amazon Prime Day (June 23–26, 2026), and the portfolio-wide WoW decline is expected post-event normalization — not a signal of underlying performance deterioration. That said, several brands warrant monitoring independent of the event: Super Mush posted a 0.71x ROAS with a 46.1% TACoS, while Hapad Canada hit a 90.4% TACoS and RationAle Brewing delivered only a 0.79x ROAS at 30.0% TACoS. Tubr Storage and Good Bacteria both carried TACoS above 60%, indicating ad spend is outpacing revenue generation at a structural level.
►
Ad Spend Efficiency — Five brands are operating below a 1.0x ROAS — Super Mush (0.71x), RationAle Brewing (0.79x), Good Bacteria (0.88x), Hapad Canada (0.86x), and Tubr Storage (0.99x) — collectively representing a meaningful drag on portfolio efficiency. Sherpa Brands and Hapad are also flagged, running at 2.49x and 1.80x ROAS respectively with TACoS of 25.1% and 24.1%, leaving limited margin headroom. Immediate bid reductions or campaign pauses on sub-1.0x ROAS brands would recover ad budget better deployed against higher-performing SKUs.
►
Actions This Week — Reduce or pause PPC spend on Super Mush, RationAle Brewing, Good Bacteria, Tubr Storage, and Hapad Canada to stop negative-ROAS bleed, and set a minimum 1.5x ROAS threshold before reinstating spend. Conduct a bid audit on Sherpa Brands to identify campaigns driving the 25.1% TACoS and reallocate budget toward top-converting ad groups to push ROAS closer to the 4.0x+ range seen in Dormeo and Opopop. Use the post-Prime Day normalization period to reset budgets, update keyword bids based on Prime Day conversion data, and establish Week 28 as the clean baseline for measuring organic recovery heading into Week 29 and 30.
