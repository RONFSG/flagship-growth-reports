Weekly Insights & Actions
►
Weekly Highlights — FSG closed Week 28 with $279,896 in total sales across 8,003 units, down 17.5% week-over-week following the conclusion of Amazon Prime Day (June 23–26, 2026). The prior week included Amazon Prime Day, and the WoW decline is expected post-event normalization, not a signal of deteriorating performance. Reshoev8er (+0.4%), Hapad (+2.9%), and Reshoev8er Canada (+6.5%) were the only brands to hold or grow sales into the post-Prime Day period, demonstrating organic demand strength outside the event window.
►
Metrics to Watch — The prior week included Amazon Prime Day (June 23–26, 2026), and the portfolio-wide WoW decline of 17.5% is expected post-event normalization and should not be treated as a negative trend signal. Super Mush (ROAS 0.77x, TACoS 44.0%), Good Bacteria (ROAS 1.09x, TACoS 59.6%), Tubr Storage (ROAS 1.13x, TACoS 59.3%), and Hapad Canada (ROAS 0.73x, TACoS 105.6%) are all generating negative ad efficiency and warrant immediate scrutiny. Hapad Canada's TACoS of 105.6% means ad spend is exceeding total revenue, making it the most urgent account to address this week.
►
Ad Spend Efficiency — The portfolio's blended ROAS of 5.88x is healthy at the top line, driven heavily by Dormeo's 7.16x ROAS, but five brands are sitting below a 2.0x ROAS threshold, pulling down overall efficiency. Super Mush, Good Bacteria, Tubr Storage, Hapad Canada, and RationAle Brewing collectively represent underperforming ad dollars that should be evaluated for bid reductions or campaign pauses. Sherpa Brands at $67,136 in revenue but only a 2.63x ROAS and 24.9% TACoS presents a significant optimization opportunity given its scale within the portfolio.
►
Actions This Week — Immediately audit and reduce bids or pause non-converting campaigns for Hapad Canada (TACoS 105.6%), Good Bacteria (TACoS 59.6%), and Tubr Storage (TACoS 59.3%) to stop spending into negative return territory. For Super Mush and RationAle Brewing, both below 1.0x ROAS, set strict spend caps and evaluate whether continued Amazon advertising investment is justified for these brands at current conversion rates. Prioritize Sherpa Brands for a full campaign structure review this week — at $67,136 in revenue, improving its 2.63x ROAS even modestly would have a meaningful impact on total portfolio efficiency.
