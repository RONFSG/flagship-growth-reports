Weekly Insights & Actions
►
Weekly Highlights — Hapad Canada closed Week 29 with $156 in total sales across 7 units, supported by $3,607 in PPC revenue and a strong ROAS of 18.24x. Tuesday July 14 was the peak sales day, suggesting mid-week shopper activity drove the majority of conversions. CVR held at 3.98%, indicating that shoppers who engaged with the listing converted at a reasonable rate.
►
Metrics to Watch — The WoW decline of -55.8% is a significant drop that warrants close monitoring to determine whether it reflects a broader demand slowdown or a temporary fluctuation. TACoS of 126.5% is notably high, meaning ad spend is far outpacing organic revenue and the business is currently reliant on PPC to generate visibility. Total organic sales of $156 against $198 in ad spend confirms the brand is spending more than it earns from direct sales alone.
►
Ad Spend Efficiency — With an ACOS of 5.5%, PPC campaigns are performing efficiently relative to ad-attributed revenue, but the $198 in spend against only $156 in total sales reveals a structural gap between ad-driven and organic performance. The high TACoS of 126.5% signals an opportunity to reduce ad dependency by improving organic rank and listing discoverability. Reviewing keyword bids and pausing low-converting terms could help reallocate budget toward higher-return placements.
►
Actions This Week — Audit active PPC campaigns to identify keywords with high spend and low conversion, and reallocate budget toward terms driving the 3.98% CVR. Work to improve organic sales velocity by reviewing listing copy, images, and pricing competitiveness to reduce reliance on ads. Set a TACoS reduction target of below 80% over the next two to three weeks as a measurable efficiency goal.
